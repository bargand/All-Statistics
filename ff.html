Market Segment: Synthetic Seed Technology

Core Principle & Definition

Synthetic seeds (synseeds) are artificially encapsulated somatic embryos, shoot buds, or cell aggregates designed to mimic natural seeds. They aim to combine clonal propagation's genetic uniformity with the handling ease of traditional seeds, enabling development into whole plants *in vitro* or *ex vitro*.

Market Segment: Cryopreservation Technology

Core Principle & Definition

Cryopreservation is the preservation of biological materials (cells, tissues, propagules) at ultra-low temperatures (typically -196¬∞C in liquid nitrogen), effectively halting all metabolic activity and enabling theoretically indefinite storage without genetic change. The primary goal is to prevent lethal intracellular ice crystal formation, often through vitrification (formation of a glassy state).

Key Cryopreservation Techniques

  • Slow Freezing: Gradual cooling before LN immersion.
  • Vitrification: Rapid cooling with high CPA concentrations.
  • Encapsulation-Dehydration: Encapsulation, sucrose preculture, desiccation, then LN.
  • Encapsulation-Vitrification: Combines encapsulation with vitrification.
  • Droplet Vitrification: Small droplets on foil for ultra-rapid cooling.

Selection depends on species, material, cost, and expertise.

Market Synergy: The Combined Powerhouse

Integrating synthetic seed technology with cryopreservation creates a powerful, layered approach. Encapsulation provides protection and a delivery system, while cryopreservation ensures indefinite, genetically stable storage. This synergy is vital for conserving species recalcitrant to other methods.
